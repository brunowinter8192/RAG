## Conclusion

**What works well:** Dense retrieval excels at single-document factual queries. Queries targeting a single, specific document (Q3, Q5, Q6, Q8, Q15) achieve 100% hit rates with the expected document at Rank 1. The retriever handles well-scoped conceptual queries with clear lexical anchors equally well (Q11, Q14). For the pgvector production-scale query (Q19), all four expected documents appear in the top 10 — demonstrating strong topical coherence across a tightly related document cluster.

**What doesn't work:** Cross-document queries requiring breadth across diverse sources are the clear weak spot. No cross-document query (Q16–Q20) achieves full recall. The retriever consistently over-represents one highly-relevant document (often the best lexical match) and misses documents that cover the same concept from a different angle. Q10 (hybrid search) and Q17 (RRF vs convex combination) are notable failures where top-10 results are dominated by a single document (Fusion_Functions_Hybrid_Retrieval.md) despite multiple other expected documents existing in the collection.

**Patterns in the misses:**

1. **Duplicate-content documents not retrieved together:** `www_anthropic_com__engineering__contextual-retrieval.md` and `www_anthropic_com__news__contextual-retrieval.md` contain near-identical content. Dense retrieval finds both when the query directly targets Contextual Retrieval (Q2, Q12), but misses both when the query is framed around a broader concept that only partially overlaps (Q10, Q17).

2. **Secondary sources consistently missed:** `docs_together_ai__docs__how-to-implement-contextual-rag-from-anthropic.md` is missing in Q10, Q12, Q17, and Q20 — queries where it is expected. It appears only once (Q20, Rank 5) when the query is directly about context enrichment techniques. The implementation tutorial is semantically downstream from the primary source and dense embeddings don't bridge this gap.

3. **`arxiv__2511.22240__pipeline-optimization.md` underperforms on cross-domain queries:** This document covers Qwen3 evaluation and reranking, but it only surfaces when queries are narrowly scoped to those topics (Q4, Q9). It is missing in Q18 (MTEB benchmarks) despite containing relevant content.

4. **`Beyond_Chunk_Then_Embed_Taxonomy.md` and `research_trychroma_com__evaluating-chunking.md` absent from Q16:** The broadest chunking query retrieves Rethinking_Chunk_Size and Zilliz chunks but misses the taxonomy paper and the Chroma evaluating-chunking paper. The taxonomy paper does appear in Q11 (late chunking) and Q20 (context enrichment) — its absence in Q16 suggests the query's framing ("fixed-size, semantic, LLM-guided") does not match its embedding space well, even though it covers all three.

5. **`docs_vectorchord_ai__vectorchord__use-case__colbert-rerank.md` never retrieved:** Not found in any of the 20 queries. Either its chunk embeddings cluster far from query space, or its indexed content is too sparse. Worth investigating whether the document was indexed correctly.
