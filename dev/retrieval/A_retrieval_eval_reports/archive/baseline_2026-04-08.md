## Observations

- **CC α=0.8 is best without reranking** — highest overall Snippet Recall (78%) and the only non-rerank mode to reach 11/20 100%-snippet queries. Zero zero-snippet queries.
- **RRF is K-insensitive** — K=30, K=60, K=90 produce identical results across all metrics and all query types.
- **CC α=0.9 regresses** — Snippet Recall falls to 72%, matching the dense baseline. Re-introduces 1 zero-snippet query. Factual Snippet Recall drops from 88% to 81%.
- **CC α=0.5/0.6** underperform on cross-document Snippet Recall (60% vs 67% for all others) and both introduce 1 zero-snippet query.
- **CC α=0.8 uniquely improves conceptual Snippet Recall** (74% vs 67% for all other non-rerank modes). All other hybrid/cc modes without rerank plateau at 67% for conceptual snippets.
- **Reranking adds +4pp Doc Recall** (80% → 84%, 12/20 → 14/20 100%-doc queries) at the cost of Snippet Recall for the best non-rerank mode (78% → 77%).
- **CC+Rerank and Hybrid+Rerank converge** — identical results across all metrics and all query types. The reranker normalizes fusion-input quality differences.
- **Cross-document queries respond differently to rerank** — Doc Recall gains +11pp (62% → 73%) but Snippet Recall loses 4pp (67% → 63%). Net trade-off depends on use case.
- **Factual queries are saturated at the hybrid/cc level** — all hybrid and cc modes (with or without rerank) achieve 100% Doc Recall and 88% Snippet Recall. Dense is the only outlier (94% / 81%).
